How Briefed works

Three steps from judgment to structured brief — no manual work, no guessing.

1
Upload your judgment
Drop in any court document — a judgment, reported case, or case notes — as a PDF, Word doc, or plain text file.
Supports PDF, DOCX, and TXT formats
Works with Australian, UK, and international cases
No account or sign-up required
2
Briefed reads the case
The document is parsed and analysed. Briefed identifies legally relevant content, attributes reasoning to specific judges, and structures everything into a consistent format.
Only information from your document is used — no guessing
Each judge's reasoning is kept separate and attributed correctly
Missing sections are clearly marked, not invented
3
Download your brief
Your structured case brief is ready to read on screen or download as a formatted PDF — suitable for tutorials, exams, and study notes.
Seven structured sections covering the full brief
Clean, formatted PDF ready to print or share
Save to your brief library for future reference
What's in your brief
01
Relevant Facts
The legally material facts — what the court actually needed to decide the case.
02
Issue
The precise legal question the court was asked to resolve.
03
Holding
The court's final answer — clear and definitive.
04
Ratio Decidendi
The controlling legal principle that makes the case binding.
05
Reasoning
How each judge applied the law to the facts, attributed by name.
06
Dissent
Any minority judgment — only included if it appears in the document.
07
Notes
Key principles and doctrinal significance for study and revision.
